Satoru Iwata

Iwata's Life:

  • 1959: Born in Sapporo, Japan.
  • 1974: Purchased an HP-65, the first programmable calculator.
  • 1975: Enters Hokkaido Sapporo South High School.
  • 1978: Obtains his first computer, a Commodore PET.
  • 1978: Got accepted into Tokyo Institute of Technology.
  • 1980: Joined HAL Labratory, Inc. as a part time employee.
  • 1982: Graduated from Tokyo Institute of Technology
  • 1982: Becomes a full time employee at HAL Labratory, Inc.
  • 1982: Releases Joust for the Nintendo Entertainment System, his first commercial game.
  • 1993: Became HAL Labratory, Inc.'s coordinator of software production.
  • 2000: Took a corporate planning division at Nintendo and a seat on the board of directors.
  • 2002: Became President of Nintendo.
  • 2003: Gave a keynote in the Tokyo Game Show.
  • 2004: Consolidated various departments created under Yamauchi.
  • 2005: Established the User Expansion Project whereby employees not normally associated with game development would submit ideas for new games.
  • 2009: Revealed the brand new Wii vitality sensor.
  • 2014: Unveiled a ten-year strategy based on quality-of-life-products.
  • 2015: Died in Kyoto Japan at the age of 55.
